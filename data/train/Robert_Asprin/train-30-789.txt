"Damnedest thing," Zaibar said, wiping at his soot-streaked face, and a moment's consternation took him. His eyes refocused. "Begging pardon, reverence-" "Got a dozen dead out there we've counted so far, just up and down the  streets. Dead men-throats cut, some; stabbed-" "The ships, Zaibar." "A  few  timbers  stove,  but  the Bey's  folk,  they  got  to  them-the bodies, reverence-a dozen of them."
