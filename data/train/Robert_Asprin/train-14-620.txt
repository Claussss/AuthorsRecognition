   Shading his eyes against the sun, Spengler peered toward the starting line where the company of Legionnaires was massing.    It wouldn't be long now. Another half hour at the most and this whole harebrained competition would be over. He assumed it wouldn't take the Legionnaires longer than that to run, the course... or give up. The Army would have its contract-and publicity-and the Eagles would have their promised night on the town.
