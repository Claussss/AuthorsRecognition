Star pulled at Samlor's arm with most of her weight. He did not look down at her, nor did his hand drop. That arm had dragged a donkey back up to the trail from which it had stumbled into a gulley a hundred feet deep. "Please," said the child. "Friend Samlor?" said the Napatan doubtfully. The knife was only that, a knife, so far as he could see. Go with him, spelled the rippling steel at which Samlor stared.
