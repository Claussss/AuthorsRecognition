"I'll be gone most of the night," he said softly. "Perhaps for the next few nights as well. I'd like it if the two of you took charge of the watch tonight. Double the guards on all the other gates, too." Dismas closed his book. "Expecting trouble?" "In this town?" He didn't need to say more. His comrades set aside their diversions and rose to follow him out. "I won't ask your business," Dismas said as they closed the door behind them. "But do you need any help?"
