Knowing they would not meet again, even in Hell-her curse being immortality. "Damn you!" Strat said, when they had gotten him onto his feet. But Crit held him there, in the cold, dusty light of dawn, a stinging wind skirling through the streets of Sanctuary, Crit had his arms about him, held him like a brother. There was no sign of the bay horse. He ached from head to foot. His knee and his elbow were bleeding, and would stiffen.
