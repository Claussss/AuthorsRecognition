If Crit was still alive at all. Ischade took the back of a chair and flung it, shoved the table back, rumpling a litter of cloaks, and simply sat down cross-legged on the floor, hands before her. Her eyes rolled back. Her lips parted. And a light grew between her hands, spinning and spinning in a way he had seen once and more than once. Like a small Globe of Power, whirling and staining her hands and her face and all the room with its cold glow.
