"Hey, this thing really has you scared, doesn't it?" Instead of answering, she rose and fled to the ladies room. Fred signaled for the check and pondered the situation. Well, he had always wondered what it would take to crack that controlled exterior. Now he knew. The waiter swept by, leaving the small black tray with the tab on it in his wake. Fred stared at it thoughtfully for a full minute, then dug out his wallet and carefully counted out a small stack of bills onto the tray. In a twinkling it disappeared with a small murmur of thanks from the waiter, and he lit a cigarette and settled down to wait.
