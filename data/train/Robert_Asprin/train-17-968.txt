'In the Upper Land.' 'My family and  yours thinned each  other's line,' stated  Kadakithis baldly, no apology, no  regret in  his words.  Yet he  looked differently  upon the  other, thinking they were of an age, both wielding wooden swords in shady courts  while the slaughter raged, far off at the fronts. 'Unto eradication,' remarked the  dark young man. 'But  we did not contest,  and now there is a different enemy, a common threat. It is enough.'
