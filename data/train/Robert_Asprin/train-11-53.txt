   "I've been enjoying this particular hunk of scenery for half a day," I complained. "I'm enjoying it so much I've memorized it."    "Don't exaggerate," my guide scolded. "This morning we were on the other side of that tree."    I closed my eyes and bit back my first five or six responses to her correction. "How long?" I repeated.    "I figure we can split after we turn that corner."    "But that corner's a good twenty-five feet away!" I protested.
