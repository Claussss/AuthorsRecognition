He didn't really care who'd tried to kill him. From the talk he'd heard while in the barracks, neither did the Stepsons: They were more concerned over walls  and the weather. He'd known that this  whole business of  putting him at  the head of  some cease fire coalition was just a roundabout way of executing him. Ritual execution, political style, wasn't a  nice way to die. But then.  Zip had killed enough to know there wasn't one.
