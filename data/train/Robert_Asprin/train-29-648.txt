"It was the hoist," came a plaintive voice from backstage. "It wasn't my fault, master-the rope slipped-" "Lempchin! You misbegotten son of a sheep-swiving Rankan!" He gathered breath, and ominous tones rolled across the stage. "What fell?" There was a silence, and Lalo bent to gather up the brushes that had been knocked from their stand. "It was ... the thunder machine." "Vashanka's rod! Do you know how much that thing cost? A gift from the Prince himself it was, and after everything-" he took a deep breath, then launched into a monologue of sorrows as eloquent as anything in the play.
