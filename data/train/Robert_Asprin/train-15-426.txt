   "Sergeant?" said Mahatma's voice, not far away. "I have a question."    "This isn't the time for questions," roared Brandy. "Get in your position and pick a target. And be ready for my signal. Do it now!"    The nervous tension along the line went up perceptibly. Out in the open, less than half a kilometer away, the ship was settling down, kicking up a cloud of dust. Brandy growled. The dust would make it harder to see what was going on. She hoped there wasn't anybody aboard that ship planning to take advantage of that momentary cover. "Hold steady," she muttered into her communicator. The ship was definitely on the ground now.
