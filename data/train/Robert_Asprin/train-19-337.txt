"Out of my face, damn you," the bard cried. "Or I'll nail what little brains you have to the back of your skull!" The thief gulped, turned, and ran, disappearing quickly into the shadows of the Maze. Sinn forgot him instantly and took in his surroundings- He was now completely disoriented and had no idea as to which way he should go to get out of the Maze and make it back to the Kettle. With a silent prayer for inspiration and direction to whatever gods would listen, the bard fled up the street and into the night.
