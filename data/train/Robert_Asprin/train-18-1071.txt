Throughout the afternoon and into the  evening he had forced any thought  of her from his mind; had, in effect, abandoned her to fate. He believed she would  not have expected, or  appreciated, anything else  and saw by  her face that  he had believed  correctly-but correctness  did nothing  to alleviate  the backlash  of self-imposed guilt which swept up around him. "Shall I leave?" Walegrin asked, piecing the situation together finally.
