Now, on this  night of the  most niggling of  crescent moons, he  stood atop the wall and took in his line from  behind and below. It stretched upward still,  to the pennon spire. It remained taut. He had to believe that it would continue  to do. Elsewise  he was  about to  splatter on  to the  pave below  like a  dropped pomegranate, a fruit whose pulp is plentiful and whose juice is red.
