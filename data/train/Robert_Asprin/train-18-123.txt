Haught, whom Straton hated, came gusting in the door. The place was softly lit and full of colors; precious gems and silks and  metals strewed the floor. Straton was, by then, the finest thing she had, though-a human man, with all his prowess, not an animated corpse or witchling. She could love him, could Ischade, with  a finer passion than the rest. But  she could feel in him a struggle, one that made shoulders sweat and muscles  twitch.
