The chill  had seeped  inward from  Haught's wet  clothes. He  felt cold, beyond shivering. He sneezed, wiped  his nose on his  sleeve, went over to  the fire to sit disconsolate. Quietly he  tried a small scrying,  to see something. Once  he had had the means, but it had  left him, with his luck; with his  freedom. 'I'll go out tomorrow,' Moria said, walking over near the fire. 'Don't,' said  Haught.
