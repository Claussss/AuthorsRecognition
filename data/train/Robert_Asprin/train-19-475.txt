The horse had died under him-once. It had come back from Hell for him. It had rescued him from enemies. Straton returned that loyalty. That small patch was the necessary Flaw-in a creature Hell had given up. But it in no wise flawed courage, or faith. Better than men, Strat thought. Better than the love of women, who had proven, overall, faithless. Critias had saved his life multiple times, too; and Strat had returned that favor, such as he could-but Crit was Crit-the ultimate pragmatist.
