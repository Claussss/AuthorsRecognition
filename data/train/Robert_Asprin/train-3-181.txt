River had  changed its  course, three  men stepped  out from  cover, barring his path, knee deep in water, crossbows drawn and blades unsheathed. A crossbow, in this wind so fierce it blotted out the Tros's snorts of  warning, and in a rain so dense no cat-gut or woman's-hair bowstring could be dry,  would shoot awry. Tempus knew it, and so  did the three who stood  there, daring him to ride  them
