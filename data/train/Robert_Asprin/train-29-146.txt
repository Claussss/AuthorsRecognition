Especially Straton- You healed the damn horse, couldn't you help me? She hurt inside. Heal him-yes. And prove to him by that, that she had not forsaken him, that there was hope for him and her. And after that, after that- She saw him lying still as all her other lovers, by morning light. It was the very fact that he loved her, that would damn him. He could not, now, take his healing as a kindness. No, to him, it would be an absolution. It would bring him to her as he had been-but more insistent, more himself, more violent and more desperate to prove his manhood after what he had suffered-
