   Sidney met the commander's gaze, and what he saw there made him decide that this was not the time to extol the virtues of freedom of the press.    "Understood, Mr. Phule," he said, giving a quick salute that wasn't entirely mockery.    Phule paid only distant attention to the antics of the photo session. Instead, he found himself watching the neighborhood rat pack of kids who interrupted their glide-board frolicking to investigate the gathering. After the reporter shooed them away from the shooting for the fifth time, this time threatening to call the police, the kids resumed their normal games, perhaps more energetically because of the nearby holophotographer.
