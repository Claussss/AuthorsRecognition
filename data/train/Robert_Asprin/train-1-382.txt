The glow was reflected faintly by the eyes of the dead youth. Khamwas's face worked in something between a grimace and a moue of embarrassment as he watched the child. "Ah," he said to Samlor. "That is, ah-are you ... ?" The caravan master shook his head, glad to find that the question amused him rather than arousing any of the other possible emotions. "On a good day," he said, "I might be able to recite a spell without stumbling over the syllables-if somebody wrote 'em out for me really careful." That was an exaggeration, though not a great one.
