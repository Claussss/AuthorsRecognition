All the tales she had ever  heard of the sorcerer yammered at her in  the voices her children had used when she told them what she meant to do. What am I doing here? Who am  I to meddle with wizards? The voices  were gentle, reasonable, and then, from some deeper part of her being came the thought:  Lalo passed through this door and came home to me. Where he has gone, I can go too.
