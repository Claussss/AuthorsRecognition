Moruth leaned forward, arms on ragged knees, grinned and looked appallingly lean and hungry- "I think we've got one what'll talk, doesn't we?" Haught flinched in his concealment beneath the bridge. Screams reached him,  not fright, but a  crescendo of them,  that was pain;  and they kept  on for a time. Then silence. He hugged himself  and shivered. They began again,  different this time, lacking distinction.
