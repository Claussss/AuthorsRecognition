"We might  have done  something while  the tie  between soul  and body was still stretching thin. But it's too late now-" "No," Mriga said. "I should have  leveled this place  the last time  I was here.  This would never have happened!" "Siveni, be still."  Mriga sat by  Harran's crushed remains,  one hand stretched out to the awful  ruin of his head;  a purposeful gesture, for  without actually touching the cold  stiff flesh, she  found herself unable  to believe in  death.
