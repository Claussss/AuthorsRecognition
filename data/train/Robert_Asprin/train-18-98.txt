Crit sat his horse, arm crooked over his pommel, and waited, watching where  his mount's ears pricked like dowsing rods. Out from the gloom  came a hand, white  and long-a woman's, despite  the leather Crit squeezed with his right knee  and the sorrel ambled forward-one pace,  two. Then he said, "Hello, Kama.
