Her eyes were grey as his horse.  Silver shot her hair, but she was  yet comely. Her hands rose, hesitated, covered a mouth pretending to hardness and tight with fear. He recognized the aborted motion other hands: towards her head,  forgetful that the rods she sought were no longer there. He did not move in his saddle, or speak again. He let her decide, glance quickly about the street, then come to him.
