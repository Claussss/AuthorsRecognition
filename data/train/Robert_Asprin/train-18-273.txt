Come on, Crit, dammit. Not a sound in the straw, not a move. He turned  around and  looked. Crit  was not  there at  all; had gone-somewhere. Upstairs again, maybe. Maybe to pass an order. Straton turned and flung the blanket on the bay, stroked its shoulder. The horse bent its head back and delicately nipped at his sleeve, nosed his ribs.
