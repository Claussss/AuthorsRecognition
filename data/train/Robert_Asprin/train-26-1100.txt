The  black chariot  stood hard  by, and as the white-robed figure  stepped down  from it,  Harran looked  up. Most carefully he sank to one knee in the dirty street, laid down the limp, bloodied young man  he was carrying, and kneeling, bowed himself slowly double. He was a priest, and  a healer, and had worked in Death's shadow before: he knew her when he saw her.
