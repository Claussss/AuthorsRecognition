The head bootlicker here was named Saliman; the hovel was reasonably  commodious once you got inside, but the band of pseudo-beggars ranged around it would  give Sync a strenuous  afternoon if he  had to cut  his way through  them to get out. He'd unbridled his horse as a precaution: if he whistled. Sync was going to have twelve hundred Rankan pounds  of iron hooves and  snapping jaws to back  him up.
