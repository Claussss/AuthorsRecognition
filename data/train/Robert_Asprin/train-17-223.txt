If no one saw us fell the tent -' Wess reached  into the  stream and  splashed her  face, cupped  her hand  in the water, and lifted it to her lips. The first rays of direct sunlight pierced  the branches and entered the clearing. Her hand was still bloody. The blood  was mixing with the water. She choked  and spat, lurched to her feet,  and bolted. A few paces  away she fell to her  knees and retched violently.
