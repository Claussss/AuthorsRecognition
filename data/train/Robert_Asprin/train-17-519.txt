He aimed anew, face set for curses rather than prayers. Elevating his bow a bit, he drew  to the  cheek and,  daringly endangering  the springy  wood, drew  even further. Uttering not a prayer but a curse, he  released. Away  sped the  arrow. It  trailed its  spidery line  Hke a strand of spittle in the pallid moonlight. It proved a night  for the heeding of  curses, if not the  answering of prayers.
