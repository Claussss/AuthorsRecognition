"You didn't know," he repeated softly to convince himself, if not the child. Gyskouras  fell  asleep once  his  sobs subsided;  the  Storm God  rages  always exhausted the small body of  their perpetrator. Molin carried an  ordinary child to a small bed where, with any luck, he would sleep for two or three days. '"Kouras can't  stay here  any longer,"  Arton said,  tugging at  the hem of the priest's much-abused tunic.
