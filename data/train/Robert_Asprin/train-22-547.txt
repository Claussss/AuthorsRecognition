But already the man was gone by; his feet scraping at the same dusty  street as  were the  feet of  a dozen  other passers  of the moment; scraping dust and breathing it in. Normally, Stulwig would have let him go. But this was not a normal time. He spun around. He jammed his stave against the ground as a brace. And took four,  long, swift steps. He reached.
