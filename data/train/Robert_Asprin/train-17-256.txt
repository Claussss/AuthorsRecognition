'Vis. Mradhon Vis.' 'Northward. A lot of places.' 'We'll talk. Tomorrow morning. Go on,  now. Believe me, that the quarrel  wasn't what it seemed.' 'Lady,' he murmured - he had known polite company once. He clenched the purse in his fist and turned off in the direction she had named - not without a  backward look. Sjekso still waited where he  had fixed himself against the wall;  but the lady seemed to know he would look back, and turned a shadowy look on him.
