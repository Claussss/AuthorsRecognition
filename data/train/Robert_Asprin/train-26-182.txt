None of  his expectations  came to  pass. Before  him, on  the other side of the carriage, were two children, one on  either side of a harried looking  woman who might once have been beautiful and whom Niko, who liked women, vaguely recalled: a temple dancer. The two children were hardly more than babes, but one of  them, the fair-haired, sat right up and clapped his little hands.
