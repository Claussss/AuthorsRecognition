A weak shaft  of light made  its way through  the jutting roofs  of a half-dozen buildings to illuminate a  pair of corpses. One  was the information broker  who had just left  Walegrin's company, a  makeshift knife still  protruding from his neck. The other was  the beggar to whom  he'd given the silver  coin. The latter bore the cleaner mark of the accomplished killer. 'I see,' Walegrin replied dully.
