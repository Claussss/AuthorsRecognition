The trees  were perhaps  fifteen or  twenty deep  from the  river's' edge.  They dismounted, removed the saddles, and hobbled the beasts again. Then they  walked through the tall cane-like plants, brushing away the flies and other pestiferous insects, until they got  to the stream itself.
