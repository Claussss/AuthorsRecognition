In the comer of the yard he found one. For fear of losing it in the dark  (since he might show no light if the root  was to work) he sat down by it,  and waited. The wind came up. Midnight struck, and with it came the mandrake's swift flower, white as  a dead  man's turned-up  eye. It  blossomed, and  shed its  cold sweet fragrance on the air, and died. Harran began to dig.
