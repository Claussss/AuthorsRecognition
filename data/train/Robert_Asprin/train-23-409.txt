"I would ask that the captains sit down.!" he ordered in a tone that left no room for rebuttal. Slowly, the forest of figures on the screen sank back into their chairs-individually, as each captain lost the battle with rebellious indignation. Tambu waited impatiently until all were seated. "Put your hands down, too!" he growled dangerously. Again, the captains complied with grudging hesitancy. "Very well. I'm going to say this once and once only. This meeting is too large for any vague semblance of democracy. With nearly two hundred of you jammed into one room, I can't even see everyone, much less recognize them to speak."
