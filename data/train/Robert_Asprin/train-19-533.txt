He was filth before his god. That was all he was, and the questions shot straight to his heart. But a second time: "What are you praying for?" the stranger asked-it was Shepherd himself. There was a watt of chill swamp air about him. "I don't know," Zip confessed, and knotted his fingers in his hair, head bowed. "I just don't know anymore ..." "Never go to a god," Shepherd said, "with preconceptions."
