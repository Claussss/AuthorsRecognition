I'm not running,  I'm not going  anywhere, just the  sun.... That, for  Haught's benefit, should he wake-with his hand on the door. The hair  stirred at  Stilcho's nape.  His flesh  crawled. He  stopped still and turned and looked, and saw Haught sitting up in the shadows, a bedraggled Haught with a bloody scrape  on his face and  the whites showing dangerously  round his eyes. Stilcho set his back against the door and gestured toward it with a shrug.
