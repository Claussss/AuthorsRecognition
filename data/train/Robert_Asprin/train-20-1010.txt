Purple  was his  armor, hawk-beaked  his helm  and tall-spiked  atop; black  his shield and the  blade of his  sword so that  there was no  gleam to announce its onrush.  Hanse  drew,  hurriedly  shifted his  buckler  into  place,  thought of Mignureal and knew he had  no time to glance aside.  Here came a god, armed  and armored, charging to end this now, right now.
