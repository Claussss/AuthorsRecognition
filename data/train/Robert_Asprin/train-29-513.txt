She looked up into Sabellia's face. The moon itself seemed a weak and helpless emberglow in the vaster dark. Tiana felt small and alone. She wanted to go home, but that, too, took money. She thought again other stonemason lover who had lured her so far from Ranke, He had treated her kindly and promised her heaven. Well, he'd given it to her. That was what the locals called this park where she now tried to ply her charms: the Promise of Heaven.
