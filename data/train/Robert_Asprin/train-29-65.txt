The thief gave no sign of recognizing Saliman, though whether this was from any cunning on his part or from simple lantern-blindness and drug-confusion, was hard to tell. Whichever it was, he decided to act before the scene had a chance to change. "Well, he's not much ... but he's the closest I've seen. I'll take him." He made a point of turning away before the slaver could even begin the anticipated protest.
