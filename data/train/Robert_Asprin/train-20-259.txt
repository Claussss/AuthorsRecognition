He heard  the moving  of the  water far  away at  his left,  heard the mutter of thunder, that confounded itself with the sounds about him. The image flashed  to him of a sodden body crucified against a pole, in the early morning rain. "Just put more men on it," the  Stepson said, never stirring from where he  sat, in the too great warmth of the room. The naivete of the operation appalled  him.
