"I'm Stilcho," he said  to them. And he  took what he carried,  a waterskin, and poured some of the contents  on the road. But it  was not water that pooled  and glistened  there. He  stepped back.  There was  a dry  rustling, a  pushing  and shoving, and something very like a  living black blanket of many pieces  settled above the glistening  puddle on the  cobbles. He backed  away and spilled  more.
