But inside everything was absolutely, utterly, unqualifiedly wrong. Jarveena wanted to cry  out, but found herself  unable to draw enough  breath. A vast sluggishness took possession other  muscles, as though she were  descending into glue.  Taking one  more step,  she knew,  would tire  her to  the point  of exhaustion; accordingly she concentrated merely on looking about her, and within seconds was wishing that she hadn't.
