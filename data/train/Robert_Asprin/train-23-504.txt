With leaden slowness, he leaned toward the mike. "Good morning. I trust everyone is well rested and ready for a full day's business?" Assorted groans and grimaces greeted his words. This had come to be a traditional opening. He knew, as they did, that the yearly meetings had become a week-long social gathering and party for the crews. That, combined with the captains' own last-minute preparations, usually guaranteed that no one arrived at the meeting well rested.
