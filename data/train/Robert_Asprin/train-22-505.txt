THE DREAM OF THE SORCERESS by A. E. Van Vogt The scream brought  Stulwig awake in  pitch darkness. He  lay for a  long moment stiff with fear. Like any resident of old, decadent Sanctuary his first fleeting thought was that the ancient city, with its night prowlers, had produced another victim's  cry of  terror. This  one was  almost as  close to  his  second-floor, greenhouse residence as- His mind paused. Realization came, then, in a nickering self-condemnation.
