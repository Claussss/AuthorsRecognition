He crossed his arms and looked down at her. At least he had that advantage: he was taller. He said; "Go on, let's hear it." "I won't tell anyone about the altar, or what's in it, as long as no perceptible trouble comes from it, if you'll give me the talisman you were going to give to it." "How do you find this crap out?" he blurted. "Is it Randal, your pet mage? You been following me? What?" She just looked up at him, her eyes full of a surety and power that her little, female body shouldn't have been able to contain, let alone radiate. It was Tempus's blood in her, some more-than-human attribute, he was certain.
