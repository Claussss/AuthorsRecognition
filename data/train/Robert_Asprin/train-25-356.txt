The sergeant sighed. "All right. Is it rigged for quartz-beams?" "The scanners say no." "Is Betsy tracking it?" "Seems to be. Shall I..." "Don't bother, I'll get it." Without raising his hat to look, the sergeant extended a leg off the hammock. The far end of his hammock was anchored on a complex mass of machinery, also covered with camouflaging. His questing toe found the firing button, which he prodded firmly. The machine hummed to life, and from its depths a beam darted out to be answered by the chill whump of an explosion in the distance.
