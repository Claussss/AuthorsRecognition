She began to  relax as the  awesome dread of  speaking with her  god passed from her. She stroked Reyk's feathers and took note of her surroundings. She had not come this far on her morning tour. The air stank of refuse and slop. Invisible life teemed: a  muffled footfall, the opening  and shutting of a  door with no light to spill through,  a choked grunt from the impenetrable  depths of an alley, mumblings, murmurings.
