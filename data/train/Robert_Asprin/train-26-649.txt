Clouds of sky-blue silk swathed her form, hiding the bruises and scratches.  Her hair was bound  in curls about  her head. Her  eyes were lightly  kohled and her cheeks  rouged to  perfection. Chenaya  could smell  the gentle  perfumes.  Most pleasing of  all was  the sun-burst  circlet, one  of her  own, that  gleamed on Daphne's brow. "I promise you'll pay for this insult," Shupansea whispered tightly.
