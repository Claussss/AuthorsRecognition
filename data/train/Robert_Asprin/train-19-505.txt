"He could spit you like a pig," she spat at him. "That man's the garrison commander, that's a Stepson, thief!" One was my lover. One was. Gods, she thought, dropping her eyes against her hand, shaking her head, I sent him away. I broke the spell, dammit, Isethimfree, there's no more spell, dammit to the hells! But it was not Crit she was thinking of. It was an anxious voice. The lights had dimmed. She looked at her young thief and saw still the scrubbed, frightened face-the knife clutched in a white-knuckled fist.
