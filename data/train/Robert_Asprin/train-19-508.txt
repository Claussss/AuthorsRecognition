His jaw set. He prepared some foolish argument, some protestation of his manhood, his impatience. She waved an arm and the door banged open, disturbing all the candles and the lamps. She let go her spell ... He stayed for nothing. He ran. She heard the gate, let him past her wards, and banged that shut and the door, clang! boom! after him. She was shaking after that. She dropped her head against her hand and tried to forget the lust that was her curse, that at times and by the pull of the moon was stronger than reason, stronger than love-
