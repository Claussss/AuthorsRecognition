"Serve your god, that he may guard you," said Tjainufi, stroking his master's-could Khamwas be called that?-right ear. "He has," Khamwas went on after the awkward pause, "a stele from my own land, from Napata-" "Of course," Samlor interrupted, placing the stranger at last. "The Land of the River." "The river," Khamwas agreed with a nod of approval, "and of the desert. And in the desert, many monuments of former times"-he paused again, gave a gentle smile-"greater times for my people, some would say, though I myself am content."
