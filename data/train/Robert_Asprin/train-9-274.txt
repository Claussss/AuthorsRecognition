   The thugs were starting to pick up boards and pieces of brick from the alley.    "Time's up!" their leader declared, starting for me.    I smiled at Kalvin.    "I think your analysis of friendship was only a little short of brilliant," I said, "There are a couple of points I'd like to go over, though."    "NOW?" the Djin shrieked. "This is hardly the time to... Look out!"    The leader of the pack was cocking his arms to take a double-handed swing at me with a piece of lumber he had acquired somewhere along the way. As the wood whistled toward its target, which is to say, my head, I made a circular gesture in the air between us with my hand... and the board rebounded as if it had struck an invisible wall!
