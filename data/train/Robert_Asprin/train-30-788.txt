"You've always mistaken me," she said. "Vampire I am not. You think it's what  I choose. I don't. But some things I can choose." Her hand closed  on his. He  leaned down and  touched her lips,  not caring, not caring to recall  or think ahead.  It was the  way he had  gone into that house. Because Ranke  might well  be through.  And he  was, soon;  and time was, he had learned in his own craft, no one's friend.
