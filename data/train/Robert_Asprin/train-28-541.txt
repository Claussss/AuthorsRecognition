Bourne squatted and, on clear  ground in the moonlight, emptied  both saddlebags in a clinking, chiming, shimmering,  glinting pile of silver coinage  amid which gleamed a few gold disks. Laboriously and without happiness, Bourne clinked them all back into the pouches of soft  leather, each the size of a nice  cushion.
