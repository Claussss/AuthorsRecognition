When she settled into the final frantic moments of the dance the sea-green  silk was  caught in  her flying  hair and  lifted away  from her  body until  it  was restrained only by the brooches at her  neck and  waist. As  she fell  into  the prostrate bow,   the silk   floated down,  hiding the   rhythmic heaving  of her exhausted lungs.  The clatter-sticks  were silent, without nagging corrections.
