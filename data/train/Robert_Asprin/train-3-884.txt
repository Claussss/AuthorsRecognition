So they told him,  the garrison commander, to keep the  guards out and alert. His men grumbled, preferring to slouch over a desultory dice game  in the barracks, but he welcomed a chance  to get away from the walls that  trapped the heat of summer as surely as they did the frigid dampness of winter.
