"I sneaked out," he said finally. "I sometimes come here alone so I can look out at my home." He sat down again and dangled his feet over the water. She sat down next to him, giving  a sidelong glance. About ten, she judged.  The note of sadness in his voice touched her. "What do you mean, your home." He pointed a small finger. "Where I come from." So, he was a Beysib child. She  could not have guessed in the absence  of light.
