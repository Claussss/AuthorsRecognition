"She didn't know how to do that before," said Samlor, as much an explanation to himself as one directed toward the other man. "She picks things up." "I see," said Khamwas, and maybe he did. "Well." He shook himself, to settle his cape and to settle himself in his resolve. "Well, Master Samtor," the Napatan continued, "I must be on." He nodded past Samlor toward the head of the alley. "Not that way," said the caravan master wryly, though he did not move again to block the other man.
