They laughed. "Who said anythin' about buyin'," the leftward one said, moving in. "Got a shock for you, citizen," the other said, with a chuckle that more resembled a giggle. "We ain't nice boys." He was moving in. "Well, y'oughtta be! Look atche-Synab's boy Hakky an' you're Saz's little brother Ahaz, ain'tche?" The youths paused to exchange a glance. "He knows us!" Ahaz whispered, high-voiced. "Shu'up," Hakky told him. "So we have to leave some work for Cholly the gluemaker, then."
