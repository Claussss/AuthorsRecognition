   Gleep, on the other hand, went where he wanted to go. While my club occasionally helped both to set him in motion and to institute minor changes in his direction once he was moving, total control had still eluded me when the day finally arrived for our departure.    The five of us (six including Gleep) gathered in the center of our practice meadow and said our goodbyes to Julie.    "I'm sorry I can't come with you, boys," he declared mournfully, "but I'm not as young as I used to be, you know?"
