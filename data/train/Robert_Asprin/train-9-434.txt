   Pookie had popped out of the taxi as soon as we stopped, so it seemed I was going to have to settle accounts with her out in the open.    "Pookie, I..."    "Heads up, Skeeve," she murmured, not looking at me. "I think we've got problems."    I followed her gaze with my eyes. Two uniformed policemen were bracketing the door to my hotel. At the sight of me, they started forward with expressions of grim determination on their faces.
