small camp in the darkness. Two bright Enlibar  wagons sat, unguarded  and garish, in  the ruddy light  of a neglected fire. Their cargo had  been scattered in tempting disarray;  chunks of aquamarine ore  shimmered in  the moonlight.  Walegrin's cloak  lay close by the fire, covering an armload of thorny sticks-a ruse to convince the brigands  that he and his men were more weary than careful and valued sleep above their lives.
