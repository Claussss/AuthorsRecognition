There must  be some  other way-he  told himself,  for however  much he hated his life, he feared death more. Human shadows slid from  the shadows behind him,  and he forced himself  to walk steadily, knowing that at  this hour, in this  part of Sanctuary, it  was indeed death to  be  visibly  afraid.  By  daylight  the  area  shared  in  the   quasi respectability of the Bazaar, but by night it belonged to the Maze.
