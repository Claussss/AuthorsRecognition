"Connections?" The tall Beysib stared with her strange eyes and fingered a small knife at the edge of her shawl of flounces. One of her necklaces moved, a  thing that had seemed cloisonne, and was not. "Connections? To whom?" "Say that this someone can save you when the walls fall." "What walls?" "Say that you serve the Beysa. Say that I serve someone else. And tell the Beysa that the wind is changing. Gold will not buy walls."
