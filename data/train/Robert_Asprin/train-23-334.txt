With effort, he closed his mind against the image. Ramona was right. He needed sleep, if only to steel his nerves. He was stretching his legs, preparing to rise from his seat, when a chime sounded and a light came on the console. Tambu smiled as he looked at the signal. Ramona was slipping. The light was red, but not blinking. Either she hadn't issued her orders yet, or a call managed to slip past her blockade.
