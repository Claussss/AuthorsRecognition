In the bright  white wizard-light, the  short fur on  the back of  his shoulders rose, He made  an answering cry,  a sighing: a  call to a  lover. He folded  his wing-fingers back along his arms. The webbing trembled and gleamed. The postillion kicked  his horse and  the cart lumbered  forward. For the  crowd outside, the show was over.
