"Hush," Moria said, "hush, Mor-am,  don't." And to Haught: "There's  food left-" It was reflex; there were times they had been hungry, she and Haught. They  were not now, and she put  on weight. She had drunk  herself stupid then; and he  had loved her  when she  had not  loved herself.  Now she  was wise  and  sober  and getting fat; and scared. "Won't you stay awhile?" -Thinking of herself alone once Mor-am went out; and terrified; and wanting  him
