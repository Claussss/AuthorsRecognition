"You," Mradhon said. "Man." The youth whirled, hand to belt, with the quick flash of steel in the blackness. "Friend," Mradhon said. He had his own knife, in case. If the young  man's mind had  been fumed, it  was shocked clear  now. He had set himself in a knifeman's crouch and Mradhon measured it as too far for any simple "Jubal," Mradhon said ever so softly. "That name make a difference to you?"
