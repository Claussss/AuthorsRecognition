"And this was not the first nightmare?" the prince was saying now. "There was only one yesterday," said Kurrekai, "but this is the third tonight, and it is not yet dawn. She will not let me try to drug her, but she must sleep. Perhaps she will listen to you." The Beysa pushed herself upright against her cushions with a sigh. "Shu-sea, love, what were you dreaming of?" The prince settled himself carefully on the foot of her bed and took her outstretched hand.
