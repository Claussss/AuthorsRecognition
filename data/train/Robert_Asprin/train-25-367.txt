He eased himself forward until he was sitting in the shallow depression, legs straight in front of him. Laying his rifle beside him, he began lifting the pieces of sod and placing them on his feet and legs, forming a solid carpet again. His head swam with pain. When he had gotten lost, his chances of survival had been low. Now they were zero. But he had gotten them all. He clung to that as he worked, lying down now and covering his bloody chest.
