So he came here, unarmed as witches and wizards reckoned such things, and looked up at the witch, and said the only thing he could say: "Let him go." Ischade held her door in her hands, a shadow against the lamplight slanting past her and reflecting off the boards. She said, "I have, Crit." "The hell!" He came up that last step onto the porch, where he towered over her. "Stop playing games'" "I assure you." She left the door standing half-open and came closer, holding her cloak about her, black velvet about bare shoulders, a whisper of silk, a waft of musk. He was sure she was naked under it-some other tryst, some other damned soul. "Leave! Now!"
