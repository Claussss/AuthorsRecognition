‘There is yet a scrap of paper which you have not read, sire,’ said
Wade, picking up a little slip which had been enclosed in the note. ‘It is a rhyming catch or the posy of a ring,’ said Monmouth, glancing
at it. ‘What are we to make of this?           “When thy star is in trine,
           Between darkness and shine,
           Duke Monmouth, Duke Monmouth,
           Beware of the Rhine!” Thy star in trine! What tomfoolery is this?’
