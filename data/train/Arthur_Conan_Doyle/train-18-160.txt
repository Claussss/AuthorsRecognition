Ane day I was workin' at the grass border when he comes up and he says,
says he: “Did ye ever have occasion tae fire a pistol, Israel?” “Godsakes!” says I, “I never had siccan a thing in my honds in my life.” “Then you'd best not begin noo,” says he. “Every man tae his ain
weepon,” he says. “Now I warrant ye could do something wi' a guid
crab-tree cudgel!” “Aye, could I,” I answered blithely, “as well as ony lad on the Border.”
