‘Nay,’ I answered, ‘human aid can do little to help me here.’ ‘There used to be a hole in the roof. Look up and see if you can see
aught of it.’ ‘It arches high above my head,’ I answered, looking upwards; ‘but there
is no sign of any opening.’ ‘There was one,’ he repeated. ‘My brother Roger hath swung himself down
wi’ a rope. In the old time the prisoners were put in so, like Joseph
into the pit. The door is but a new thing.’
