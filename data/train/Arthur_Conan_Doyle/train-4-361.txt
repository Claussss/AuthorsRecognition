‘Did I hear aright?’ he asked. ‘Is your name Clarke?’ ‘It is,’ I answered. ‘Your Christian name?’ ‘Living at?’ The clergyman conferred for a few moments with a grizzly-bearded,
harsh-faced man dressed in black buckram who stood at his elbow. ‘If you are really Micah Clarke of Havant,’ quoth he, ‘you will be able
to tell us the name of an old soldier, skilled in the German wars, who
was to have come with ye to the camp of the faithful.’
