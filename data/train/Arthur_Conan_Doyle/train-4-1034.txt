‘What hast thou to do?’ he snarled. ‘Let go!’ ‘Nay, nay! Blood enough hath been shed,’ said I. ‘Let them lie.’ ‘What mercy would they have had upon us?’ he cried passionately,
struggling to get his wrist free. ‘They have lost, and must pay
forfeit.’ ‘Not in cold blood,’ I said firmly. ‘I shall not abide it.’ ‘Indeed, your lordship,’ he sneered, with the devil peeping out through
his eyes. With a violent wrench he freed himself from my grasp, and
springing back, picked up the sword which he had dropped.
