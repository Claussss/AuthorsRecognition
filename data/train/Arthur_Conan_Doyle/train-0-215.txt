“Neither would I,” said her companion. “You! Well, I don’t see that it would make much matter to you, anyhow.
You ain’t even a friend of ours.” The young hunter’s dark face grew so gloomy over this remark that Lucy
Ferrier laughed aloud. “There, I didn’t mean that,” she said; “of course, you are a friend now.
You must come and see us. Now I must push along, or father won’t trust
me with his business any more. Good-bye!”
