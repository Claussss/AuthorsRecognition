‘An alibi, your Lordship.’ ‘Ha! The common plea of every scoundrel. Have they witnesses?’ ‘We have here a list of forty witnesses, your Lordship. They are waiting
below, many of them having come great distances, and with much toil and
trouble.’ ‘Who are they? What are they?’ cried Jeffreys. ‘They are country folk, your Lordship. Cottagers and farmers, the
neighbours of these poor men, who knew them well, and can speak as to
their doings.’
