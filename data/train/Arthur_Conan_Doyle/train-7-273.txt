The Emir spoke again in his harsh, rasping voice. “He says,” Mansoor translated, “that the baggage-camels are spent, and
that there is only one beast left which can keep up. It is ready now for
one of you, and you have to decide among yourselves which is to have it.
If one is richer than the others, he will have the preference.” “Tell him that we are all equally rich.” “In that case he says that you are to choose at once which is to have
the camel.”
