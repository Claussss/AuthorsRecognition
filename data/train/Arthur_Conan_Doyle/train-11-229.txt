“The word of a Portuguese gentleman is sufficient,” said he. “Not a word will I say until you have sworn it.” He flushed with anger and his eyes swept round toward the saw. But he
understood from my tone that I meant what I said, and that I was not
a man to be bullied into submission. He pulled a cross from under his
zammara or jacket of black sheepskin. “I swear it,” said he. Oh, my joy as I heard the words! What an end--what an end for the first
swordsman of France! I could have laughed with delight at the thought.
