“One more fall, by all the saints!” he cried, throwing out his arms. “Not I,” quoth the archer, pulling on his clothes, “I have come well out
of the business. I would sooner wrestle with the great bear of Navarre.” “It was a trick,” cried John. “Aye was it. By my ten finger-bones! it is a trick that will add a
proper man to the ranks of the Company.” “Oh, for that,” said the other, “I count it not a fly; for I had
promised myself a good hour ago that I should go with thee, since the
life seems to be a goodly and proper one. Yet I would fain have had the
feather-bed.”
