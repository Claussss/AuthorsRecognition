“What can be the matter that he does not come?” she said. “It is the
first day since our engagement that I have not seen him.” Robert looked out through the window. “It is a gusty night, and raining hard,” he remarked. “I do not at all
expect him.” “Poor Hector used to come, rain, snow, or fine. But, then, of course, he
was a sailor. It was nothing to him. I hope that Raffles is not ill.” “He was quite well when I saw him this morning,” answered her brother,
and they relapsed into silence, while the rain pattered against the
windows, and the wind screamed amid the branches of the elms outside.
