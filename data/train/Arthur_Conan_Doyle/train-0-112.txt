“The plot thickens,” he said, as I entered; “I have just had an answer
to my American telegram. My view of the case is the correct one.” “And that is?” I asked eagerly. “My fiddle would be the better for new strings,” he remarked. “Put your
pistol in your pocket. When the fellow comes speak to him in an ordinary
way. Leave the rest to me. Don’t frighten him by looking at him too
hard.” “It is eight o’clock now,” I said, glancing at my watch.
