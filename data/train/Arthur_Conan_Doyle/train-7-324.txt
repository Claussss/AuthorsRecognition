“But the others?” he asked, his face turning grave again. The Colonel shook his head. “We left them behind at the wells. I fear
that it is all over with them.” “Tut, tut!” cried the clergyman, in a boisterous voice, which could not
cover the despondency of his expression; “you thought, no doubt, that
it was all over with me, but here I am in spite of it. Never lose heart,
Mrs. Belmont. Your husband's position could not possibly be as hopeless
as mine was.”
