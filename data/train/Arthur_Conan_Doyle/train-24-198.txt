“Until the sun is over the great lime-tree, good master,” the elder
answered. “What would ye be, then? Woodmen? Verderers?” “Nay, soldiers,” they cried both together. “By the beard of my father! but ye are whelps of the true breed. Why so
keen, then, to be soldiers?” “That we may fight the Scots,” they answered. “Daddy will send us to
fight the Scots.” “And why the Scots, my pretty lads? We have seen French and Spanish
galleys no further away than Southampton, but I doubt that it will be
some time before the Scots find their way to these parts.”
