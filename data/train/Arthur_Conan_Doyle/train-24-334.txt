“I take shame,” said Alleyne, “that I should have touched it.” “Nay, it warmed my very heart to see it. So glad was I, that I laughed
for very pleasure. My fine preacher can himself be tempted then, thought
I; he is not made of another clay to the rest of us.” “God help me! I am the weakest of the weak,” groaned Alleyne. “I pray
that I may have more strength.” “And to what end?” she asked sharply. “If you are, as I understand, to
shut yourself forever in your cell within the four walls of an abbey,
then of what use would it be were your prayer to be answered?”
