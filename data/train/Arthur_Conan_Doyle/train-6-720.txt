“By the rood of Beverley!” cried old Wat, “I have not seen a finer
roving shaft this many a year. In my own best day I could not have
bettered it. Which of you loosed it?” “It was Aylward of Tilford--Samkin Aylward,” cried a score of voices,
and the bowman, flushed at his own fame, was pushed to the front. “Indeed I would that it had been at a nobler mark,” said he. “He might
have gone free for me, but I could not keep my fingers from the string
when he turned to jeer at us.”
