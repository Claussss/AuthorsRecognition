The words were hardly out of my mouth when the general dropped upon his
knees with his long, thin arms extended to Heaven. “Thy will be done!” he cried in a cracking voice. “Thy blessed will be
done!” I could see through the crack that Corporal Rufus Smith's face had
turned to a sickly yellow shade, and that he was wiping the perspiration
from his brow. “It's like my luck!” he said. “After all these years, to come when I
have got a snug billet.”
