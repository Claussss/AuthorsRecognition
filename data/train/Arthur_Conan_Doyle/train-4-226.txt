‘My poor beast is nearly done for,’ Reuben remarked, leaning forward and
passing his hand down the creature’s reeking neck. ‘For all that we cannot rest,’ said Saxon. ‘We may not be out of danger
yet. Another mile or two may shake us clear. But I like it not.’ ‘Like not what?’ ‘These horses and their terrors. The beasts can at times both see and
hear more than we, as I could show by divers examples drawn from mine
own experience on the Danube and in the Palatinate, were the time and
place more fitting. Let us on, then, before we rest.’
