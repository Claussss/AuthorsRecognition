‘Jinny, the cow, is just across the marsh,’ quoth she. ‘It is nigh
milking time, and I shall fetch her round if ye wish more.’ ‘Now, God forbid!’ cried Reuben. ‘It will end in our having to buy the
cow. Where is your granny, little maid?’ ‘She hath gone into the town,’ the child answered. ‘There are bad men
with red coats and guns coming to steal and to fight, but granny will
soon make them go ‘way. Granny has gone to set it all right.’
