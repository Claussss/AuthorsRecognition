“Oh, that’s all right,” he cried, with a merry laugh. “I think we may
consider the thing as settled--that is, if the rooms are agreeable to
you.” “When shall we see them?” “Call for me here at noon to-morrow, and we’ll go together and settle
everything,” he answered. “All right--noon exactly,” said I, shaking his hand. We left him working among his chemicals, and we walked together towards
my hotel.
