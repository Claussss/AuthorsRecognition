“Ha, John!” cried the prince, craning his neck, “who is this cavalier,
and what is it that he desires?” “On my word, sire,” replied Chandos, with the utmost surprise upon his
face, “it is my opinion that he is a Frenchman.” “A Frenchman!” repeated Don Pedro. “And how can you tell that, my Lord
Chandos, when he has neither coat-armor, crest, or blazonry?” “By his armor, sire, which is rounder at elbow and at shoulder than any
of Bordeaux or of England. Italian he might be were his bassinet more
sloped, but I will swear that those plates were welded betwixt this and
Rhine. Here comes his squire, however, and we shall hear what strange
fortune hath brought him over the marches.”
