"Right, so far," said I.  "Anything else?" "He was a man of untidy habits,--very untidy and careless.  He was left
with good prospects, but he threw away his chances, lived for some time
in poverty with occasional short intervals of prosperity, and finally,
taking to drink, he died.  That is all I can gather." I sprang from my chair and limped impatiently about the room with
considerable bitterness in my heart.
