“Gerard!” said he; “Gerard!” I could but look my sympathy, but he, though the life was ebbing swiftly
out of him, still kept his duty before him, like the gallant gentleman
he was. “The beacon, Gerard! You will light it?” “Have you flint and steel?” “It is here!” “Then I will light it to-night.” “I die happy to hear you say so. They shot me, Gerard. But you will tell
the Marshal that I did my best.”
