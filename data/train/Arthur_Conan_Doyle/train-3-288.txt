"'Scuse me, sir!" said he, looking at me with a glassy stare, "but you
bear most 'straordinary resemblance Olver Lodge." I said something amiable. "Yes, sir--'straordinary! Have you ever seen Olver Lodge, sir?" "Yes, I have." "Well, did you perceive resemblance?" "Sir Oliver, as I remember him, was a tall man with a grey beard." He shook his head at me sadly. "No, sir--I heard him at Wellington last week. No beard. A moustache,
sir, same as your own."
