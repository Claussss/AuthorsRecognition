      The young woman turned over a sheaf of counterfoils.       “What o’clock was it?” she asked.       “A little after six.”       “Whom was it to?”       Holmes put his finger to his lips and glanced at me. “The last
      words in it were ‘For God’s sake,’” he whispered, confidentially;
      “I am very anxious at getting no answer.”       The young woman separated one of the forms.       “This is it. There is no name,” said she, smoothing it out upon
      the counter.
