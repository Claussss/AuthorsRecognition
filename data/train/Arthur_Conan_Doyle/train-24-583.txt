“And what of me, young sir?” “Ma foi! this is my lady's page come over. Your cheek will be browner
and your hand harder ere you see your mother again.” “If my hand is not hard, it is ready.” “Ready? Ready for what? For the hem of my lady's train?” “Ready to chastise insolence, sir,” cried Alleyne with flashing eyes. “Sweet little coz!” answered the burly squire. “Such a dainty color!
Such a mellow voice! Eyes of a bashful maid, and hair like a three
years' babe! Voila!” He passed his thick fingers roughly through the
youth's crisp golden curls.
