“If it were not for your grey hairs----” he said. “Damn your impudence!” cried the Colonel. “If we have to die, let us die like gentlemen, and not like so many
corner-boys,” said Belmont, with dignity. “I only said I was glad to see that Monsieur Fardet had learned
something from his adventures,” the Colonel sneered. “Shut up, Cochrane! What do you want to aggravate him for?” cried the
Irishman. “Upon my word, Belmont, you forget yourself! I do not permit people to
address me in this fashion.”
