"Why do you follow me?" he asked. "It is my hope, Cæsar, that I may always follow you." His flushed face
as he spoke was almost level with that of the mounted Roman. "By the god of war, I do not know where in all the world I could find
such a servant!" cried the Emperor. "You shall be my own body-guard,
the one nearest to me of all." The giant fell upon his knee. "My life and strength are yours," he said.
"I ask no more than to spend them for Cæsar."
