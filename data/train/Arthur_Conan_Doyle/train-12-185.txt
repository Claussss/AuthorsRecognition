“By the way, Robert,” said Raffles Haw suddenly, as they walked up the
Avenue. “Has your great Roman picture gone to London?” “I have not finished it yet.” “But I know that you are a quick worker. You must be nearly at the end
of it.” “No, I am afraid that it has not advanced much since you saw it. For one
thing, the light has not been very good.” Raffles Haw said nothing, but a pained expression flashed over his face.
When they reached the house he led the way through the museum. Two great
metal cases were lying on the floor.
