"Gladys!" I cried, "Gladys!" She looked up with amazement in her face.  She was altered in some
subtle way.  The expression of her eyes, the hard upward stare, the set
of the lips, was new to me.  She drew back her hands. "What do you mean?" she said. "Gladys!" I cried.  "What is the matter?  You are my Gladys, are you
not--little Gladys Hungerton?" "No," said she, "I am Gladys Potts.  Let me introduce you to my
husband."
