“Whistle him off, sire! Whistle off the gerfalcon!” cried the Bishop. “Nay, nay, he is overfar. She would fly at check.” “Now, sire, now!” cried the Prince, as the great bird with the breeze
behind him came sweeping down the sky. The King gave the shrill whistle, and the well-trained hawk raked out to
the right and to the left to make sure which quarry she was to follow.
Then, spying the heron, she shot up in a swift ascending curve to meet
him.
