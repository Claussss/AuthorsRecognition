“I would fain have your rede, Sir Oliver, for Master Hawtayne hath fears
that when we veer there may come danger from the hole in our side.” “Then do not veer,” quoth Sir Oliver hastily. “And now, fair sir, I must
hasten back to see how my rogues have fared with the brawn.” “Nay, but this will scarce suffice,” cried the shipman. “If we do not
veer we will be upon the rocks within the hour.” “Then veer,” said Sir Oliver. “There is my rede; and now, Sir Nigel, I
must crave----”
