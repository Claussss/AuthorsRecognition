“It might have been no laughing matter for us,” the other remarked
petulantly. “I have lost a good sea-kit and nearly my life into the
bargain.” “Do I understand you to say,” said I, “that you attribute your
misfortunes to your ill-fated passengers?” The mate opened his eyes at the adjective. “Why ill-fated, sir?” he asked. “Because they are most certainly drowned,” I answered. He sniffed incredulously and went on warming his hands.
