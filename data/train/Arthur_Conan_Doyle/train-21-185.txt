A Break in the Chain It was late in the afternoon before I woke, strengthened and refreshed.
Sherlock Holmes still sat exactly as I had left him, save that he had
laid aside his violin and was deep in a book. He looked across at me,
as I stirred, and I noticed that his face was dark and troubled. "You have slept soundly," he said.  "I feared that our talk would wake
you." "I heard nothing," I answered.  "Have you had fresh news, then?"
