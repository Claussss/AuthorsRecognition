He stared at me with a hopeless expression upon his vacuous,
good-natured, scrubby little face. "Don't you think all this is a little too personal?" he said. "Well, just one question," I cried.  "What are you?  What is your
profession?" "I am a solicitor's clerk," said he.  "Second man at Johnson and
Merivale's, 41 Chancery Lane." "Good-night!" said I, and vanished, like all disconsolate and
broken-hearted heroes, into the darkness, with grief and rage and
laughter all simmering within me like a boiling pot.
