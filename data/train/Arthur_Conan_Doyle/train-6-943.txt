“How long is it, my Lord Chandos,” said he hotly, “since you have taken
it upon yourself to wear my arms?” Chandos smiled. “It is surely you who have mine,” said he, “since this
surcoat was worked for thee by the good nuns of Windsor a long year
ago.” “If it were not for the truce,” said Clermont, “I would soon show you
that you have no right to wear it.” “Look for it then in the battle to-morrow, and I also will look for
yours,” Chandos answered. “There we can very honorably settle the
matter.”
