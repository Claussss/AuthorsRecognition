Von Bork had mastered his anger. “We have been allies too long to quarrel now at the very hour of
victory,” he said. “You’ve done splendid work and taken risks, and I
can’t forget it. By all means go to Holland, and you can get a boat
from Rotterdam to New York. No other line will be safe a week from now.
I’ll take that book and pack it with the rest.” The American held the small parcel in his hand, but made no motion to
give it up.
