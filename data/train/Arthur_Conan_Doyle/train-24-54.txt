“I am a clerk on my road from Beaulieu to Minstead.” “Aye, indeed! Hast been brought up at the Abbey then. I could read it
from thy reddened cheek and downcast eye. Hast learned from the monks, I
trow, to fear a woman as thou wouldst a lazar-house. Out upon them! that
they should dishonor their own mothers by such teaching. A pretty world
it would be with all the women out of it.” “Heaven forfend that such a thing should come to pass!” said Alleyne.
