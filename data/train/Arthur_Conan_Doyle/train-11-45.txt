“I had not forgotten it,” the old man answered. “Brother, if the tribunal has injured you in one direction, it will give
you ample satisfaction in another.” The young man who had been pleading when I entered the room staggered to
his feet. “I cannot endure it,” he cried. “Your Excellency must forgive me. The
tribunal can act without me. I am ill. I am mad.” He flung his hands out
with a furious gesture and rushed from the room.
