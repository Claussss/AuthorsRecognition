‘A day of this child would sicken me for over of soldiering,’ Reuben
answered. ‘The cavalier and the butcher become too near of kin, as I
listen to her.’ ‘Perhaps both are equally needful,’ said I, shrugging my shoulders. ‘We
have put our hands to the plough. But methinks I see the man for whom we
wait coming down under the shadow of yonder line of pollard willows.’ ‘It is he, sure enough,’ cried Reuben, peeping through the diamond-paned
window.
