"Poor Sir William!" laughed Lady Clara.  "But how has he roused your
wrath?" "He is so persistent-so dogmatic." "Upon what point?" "Well, he has been laying down the law about Ida.  He has decreed, it
seems, that she is to go to Tangier." "He said something to that effect before he went up to you." "Oh, he did, did he?" The slow-moving, inscrutable eye came sliding round to her. Lady Clara's face had assumed an expression of transparent obvious
innocence, an intrusive candour which is never seen in nature save when
a woman is bent upon deception.
