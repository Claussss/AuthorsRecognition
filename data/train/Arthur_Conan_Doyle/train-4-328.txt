‘One who hath seen much service abroad,’ I answered. ‘Ha! ye are lucky to ride to the wars in the company of so accomplished
a cavalier. For I presume that it is to the wars that ye are riding,
since ye are all so armed and accoutred.’ ‘We are indeed bound for the West,’ I replied, with some reserve, for in
Saxon’s absence I did not care to be too loose-tongued. ‘And in what capacity?’ he persisted. ‘Will ye risk your crowns in
defence of King James’s one, or will ye strike in, hit or miss, with
these rogues of Devon and Somerset? Stop my vital breath, if I would not
as soon side with the clown as with the crown, with all due respect to
your own principles!’
