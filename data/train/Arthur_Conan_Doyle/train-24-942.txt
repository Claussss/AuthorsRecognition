“It is a mark that I have found before now,” answered the young bowman. “And shall again, camarade, I doubt not. But hola! Johnston, who is this
who holds his bow like a crow-keeper?” “It is Silas Peterson, of Horsham. Do not wink with one eye and look
with the other, Silas, and do not hop and dance after you shoot, with
your tongue out, for that will not speed it upon its way. Stand straight
and firm, as God made you. Move not the bow arm, and steady with the
drawing hand!”
