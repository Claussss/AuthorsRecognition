'Not guilty, my lord,' said I. '_Vox populi, vox Dei._ You are acquitted, Captain Crocker. So long as
the law does not find some other victim you are safe from me. Come back
to this lady in a year, and may her future and yours justify us in the
judgment which we have pronounced this night!' 
THE PRIZE LODGER By George Gissing (_Human Odds and Ends/Stories and Sketches_, London: Lawrence and Bullen
Ltd, 1898)
