      “My God, what’s that, Watson?”       “I don’t know. It’s a sound they have on the moor. I heard it
      once before.”       It died away, and an absolute silence closed in upon us. We stood
      straining our ears, but nothing came.       “Watson,” said the baronet, “it was the cry of a hound.”       My blood ran cold in my veins, for there was a break in his voice
      which told of the sudden horror which had seized him.
