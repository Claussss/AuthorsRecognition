“May I ask, sir,” said I to the major, “at what hour the regiment is
paraded?” “I trust, Captain Gerard, that you do not mean to alter our hours,” said
he, and again there was a burst of laughter, which died away as I looked
slowly round the circle. “What hour is the assembly?” I asked, sharply, of Captain Pelletan. Some mocking answer was on his tongue, but my glance kept it there. “The
assembly is at six,” he answered.
