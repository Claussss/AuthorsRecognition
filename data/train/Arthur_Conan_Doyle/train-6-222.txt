“This is fond talk and can lead to nothing, fair sir,” said he, turning
away and fiddling with the keys of his strong boxes. “Yet I have no wish
to be hard on you. Take my outside price, which is fifty nobles.” “And a hundred,” whispered Aylward. “And a hundred,” said Nigel, blushing at his own greed. “Well, well, take a hundred!” cried the merchant. “Fleece me, skin me,
leave me a loser, and take for your wares the full hundred!”
