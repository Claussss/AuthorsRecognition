‘Not a word, my lord,’ I replied. ‘And his private message was that he wished us well, and would join us
if we were in his country?’ ‘That was the effect, my lord.’ ‘Yet in his council, as I understand, he did utter bitter things against
us, putting affronts upon the King, and making light of his just claims
upon the fealty of his nobility?’ ‘He did,’ I answered. ‘He would fain stand upon both sides of the hedge at once,’ said King
Monmouth. ‘Such a man is very like to find himself on neither side, but
in the very heart of the briars. It may be as well, however, that we
should move his way, so as to give him the chance of declaring himself.’
