"Miss Cameron, I believe," said I. "I am your fellow-lodger. Upperton is
my name. We must introduce ourselves in these wilds if we are not to be
for ever strangers." "Oh, then, you live also with Mrs. Adams!" she cried. "I had thought
that there were none but peasants in this strange place." "I am a visitor, like yourself," I answered. "I am a student, and have
come for quiet and repose, which my studies demand."
