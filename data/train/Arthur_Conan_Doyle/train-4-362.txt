‘Why, this is he,’ I answered; ‘Decimus Saxon is his name.’ ‘Aye, aye, Master Pettigrue,’ cried the old man. ‘The very name given by
Dicky Rumbold. He said that either the old Roundhead Clarke or his son
would go with him. But who are these?’ ‘This is Master Reuben Lockarby, also of Havant, and Sir Gervas Jerome
of Surrey,’ I replied. ‘They are both here as volunteers desiring to
serve under the Duke of Monmouth.’
