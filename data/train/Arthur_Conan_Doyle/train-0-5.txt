“Whatever have you been doing with yourself, Watson?” he asked in
undisguised wonder, as we rattled through the crowded London streets.
“You are as thin as a lath and as brown as a nut.” I gave him a short sketch of my adventures, and had hardly concluded it
by the time that we reached our destination. “Poor devil!” he said, commiseratingly, after he had listened to my
misfortunes. “What are you up to now?”
