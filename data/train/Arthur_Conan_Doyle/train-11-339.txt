His supercilious manner was more than I could endure. He had evidently spoken French to the lady in order that I might
understand the humiliating way in which he referred to me. “I will take no favour from you,” said I. “You may do what you like, but
I will never give you my parole.” The Russian shrugged his great shoulders, and turned away as if the
matter were ended. “Very well, my fine fellow, so much the worse for your fingers and toes.
We shall see how you are in the morning after a night in the snow.”
