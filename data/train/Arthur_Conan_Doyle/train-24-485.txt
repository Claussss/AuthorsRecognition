The pirate leader had stood by the bulwarks, a cord round his arms,
and two stout archers on either side. At Sir Nigel's words he started
violently, and his swarthy features blanched to a livid gray. “How, Sir Knight?” he cried in broken English. “Que dites vous? To hang,
le mort du chien! To hang!” “It is my vow,” said Sir Nigel shortly. “From what I hear, you thought
little enough of hanging others.”
