“Or ill,” said my sister gloomily. “Why, what a little croaker you are, to be sure!” I cried. “What in the
world is coming over you?” “I feel nervous and low-spirited,” she answered, drawing closer to my
side and shivering. “I feel as if some great peril were hanging over the
heads of those we love. Why should these strange men wish to stay upon
the coast?” “What, the Buddhists?” I said lightly. “Oh, these fellows have continual
feast-days and religious rites of all sorts. They have some very good
reason for staying, you may be sure.”
