“I do believe that you are all the time enjoying it, Mr. Stephens,” said
Sadie, with some bitterness. “I would not go so far as to say that,” he answered. “But I am quite
certain that I would not leave you here.” [Illustration: Certain that I would not leave you here p152] It was the nearest approach to tenderness which he had ever put into a
speech, and the girl looked at him in surprise. “I think I've been a very wicked girl all my life,” she said, after a
pause. “Because I have had a good time myself, I never thought of those
who were unhappy. This has struck me serious. If ever I get back I shall
be a better woman--a more earnest woman--in the future.”
