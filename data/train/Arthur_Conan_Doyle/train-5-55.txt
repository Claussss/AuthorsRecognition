"Ah, Mr. Brewster!  Better to-day?" he asked. "Come in, doctor!  Yes, I'm better.  But there's a deal o' bubbling in
my chest.  It's all them toobes.  If I could but cut the phlegm, I'd be
right.  Can't you give me something to cut the phlegm?" The doctor, a grave-faced young man, put his fingers to the furrowed,
blue-corded wrist. "You must be careful," he said.  "You must take no liberties."  The
thin tide of life seemed to thrill rather than to throb under his
finger.
