"Now then," said Brown, facing round, and brushing the crumbs from his
lap, "who is to put it on?" "I shall," cried his companion eagerly. "What we do to-night is likely
to be historic." "But there is some danger," suggested Brown. "We cannot quite tell how
it may act." "That is nothing," said Pericord, with a wave of his hand. "But there is no use our going out of our way to incur danger." "What then? One of us must do it."
