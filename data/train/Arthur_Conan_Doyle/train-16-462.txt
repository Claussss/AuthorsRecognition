It was not a very gracious speech, but it was a look of pity and not of
anger which shone in her eyes as she turned away from his bedside. Doctor Ripley had a brother, William, who was assistant surgeon at a
London hospital, and who was down in Hampshire within a few hours of his
hearing of the accident. He raised his brows when he heard the details. "What! You are pestered with one of those!" he cried.
