I was about to point this out to him, but at the first word I uttered he
gave a shout of astonishment, and, rushing out, he seized the lamp which
hung in the corridor and flashed it in my face. “It is you, you villain!” he cried. “You French coxcomb. You shall pay
me for the wrong which you have done me.” But the next instant he saw the pallor of my face and the blood which
was still pouring from my head.
