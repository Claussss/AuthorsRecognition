‘I am prepared to find that your judgment is the correct one,’ said Sir
Gervas Jerome. ‘I have learned to distrust too hasty dislikes, and bear
in mind that couplet of John Dryden--           “Errors, like straws, upon the surface flow.
           He who would search for pearls must dive below.”’ ‘Or worthy Dr. Samuel Butler,’ said Saxon, ‘who, in his immortal poem of
“Hudibras,” says--           “The fool can only see the skin:
           The wise man tries to peep within.”’
