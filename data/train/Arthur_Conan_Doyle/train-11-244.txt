“This is the way down,” said he, and then, suddenly, “Dios mio, what is that?” A terrible cry had risen out of the woods beneath us. I saw that de Pombal was shivering like a frightened horse. “It is that devil,” he whispered. “He is treating another as he treated
me. But on, on, for Heaven help us if he lays his hands upon us.” One by one we crawled down the narrow goat track. At the bottom of the cliff we were back in the woods once more. Suddenly
a yellow glare shone above us, and the black shadows of the tree-trunks
started out in front.
