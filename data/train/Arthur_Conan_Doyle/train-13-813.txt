      It may have been a little over an hour before I reached
      Meiringen. Old Steiler was standing at the porch of his hotel.       “Well,” said I, as I came hurrying up, “I trust that she is no
      worse?”       A look of surprise passed over his face, and at the first quiver
      of his eyebrows my heart turned to lead in my breast.       “You did not write this?” I said, pulling the letter from my
      pocket. “There is no sick Englishwoman in the hotel?”
