“Where do you put up in Cairo, Miss Adams?” asked Mrs. Belmont, at last. “Shepheard's, I think.” “And you, Mr. Stephens?” “Oh, Shepheard's, decidedly.” “We are staying at the Continental. I hope we shall not lose sight of
you.” “I don't want ever to lose sight of you, Mrs. Belmont,” cried Sadie.
“Oh, you must come to the States, and we'll give you just a lovely
time.” Mrs. Belmont laughed, in her pleasant, mellow fashion.
