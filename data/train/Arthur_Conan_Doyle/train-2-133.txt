“We have quite a little round of afternoon calls to make,” said he. “I
think that Sir James Walter claims our first attention.” The house of the famous official was a fine villa with green lawns
stretching down to the Thames. As we reached it the fog was lifting,
and a thin, watery sunshine was breaking through. A butler answered our
ring. “Sir James, sir!” said he with solemn face. “Sir James died this
morning.”
