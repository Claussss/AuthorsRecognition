“Tell me, Aylward,” said Alleyne, “what was amiss with the door of
yonder inn that you should ask me to observe it.” “Pardieu! yes, I had well-nigh forgot. What saw you on yonder door?” “I saw a square hole, through which doubtless the host may peep when he
is not too sure of those who knock.” “And saw you naught else?” “I marked that beneath this hole there was a deep cut in the door, as
though a great nail had been driven in.”
