She tied the coin hurriedly into the corner of her rags, as though she
feared that I might try to wrest it from her. ‘It will buy bread,’ she
croaked. ‘But who is there to sell it, good mistress?’ I asked. ‘They sell it at Fovant, and they sell it at Hindon,’ she answered. ‘I
bide here o’ days, but I travel at night.’ ‘I warrant she does, and on a broomstick,’ quoth Saxon; ‘but tell us,
mother, who is it who hangs above your head?’
