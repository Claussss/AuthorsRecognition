The Barbarian rose, and his fair cheeks flushed with resentment. "I will serve Cæsar as a soldier," said he, "but I will be house-servant
to no man--not even to him. If Cæsar would see what manner of man I am,
let him put one of his guardsmen up against me." "By the shade of Milo this is a bold fellow!" cried the Emperor. "How
say you, Crassus? Shall he make good his words?" "By your leave, Cæsar," said the blunt soldier, "good swordsmen are too
rare in these days that we should let them slay each other for sport.
Perhaps if the Barbarian would wrestle a fall----"
