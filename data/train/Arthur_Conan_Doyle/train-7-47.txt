“Sorry your wife isn't coming, Belmont,” said the Colonel. “I think she had a touch of the sun yesterday. Her head aches very
badly.” His voice was strong and thick like his figure. “I should stay to keep her company, Mr. Belmont,” said the little
American old maid; “but I learn that Mrs. Shlesinger finds the ride too
long for her, and has some letters which she must mail to-day, so Mrs.
Belmont will not be lonesome.”
