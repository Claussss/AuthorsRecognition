Sir Robert Knolles was pacing the deck in the early morning, when he
heard the sound of oars, and there were his two night-birds climbing up
the side. “So, fellow,” said he, “have you had speech with the King of Sark?” “Fair sir, I have seen him.” “And he has paid his forfeit?” “He has paid it, sir!” Knolles looked with curiosity at the bag which Simon bore. “What carry
you there?” he asked. “The stake that he has lost.”
