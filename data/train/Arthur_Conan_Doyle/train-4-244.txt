‘Why, to recover my mare if it can anywise be done,’ said the
adventurer.’ Though on this vast moor, in the dark, she will be as
difficult to find as a Scotsman’s breeches or a flavourless line in
“Hudibras.”’ ‘And Reuben Lockarby’s steed can go no further,’ I remarked. ‘But do
mine eyes deceive me, or is there a glimmer of light over yonder?’ ‘A Will-o’-the-wisp,’ said Saxon.           “An _ignis fatuus_ that bewitches,
           And leads men into pools and ditches.”
