Stephens shook his head in silence. He had seen the death of the
donkey-boys, and she had not. “Maybe we are sent to guide them into a better path,” said the old lady.
“Maybe we are specially singled out for a good work among them.” If it were not for her niece her energetic and enterprising temperament
was capable of glorying in the chance of evangelising Khartoum, and
turning Omdurman into a little well-drained, broad-avenued replica of a
New England town.
