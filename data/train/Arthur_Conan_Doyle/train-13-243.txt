      “‘He would rather have our small price than your big one,’ said
      he.       “‘I’ll lay you a fiver,’ said I, ‘that when he has my offer
      you’ll never so much as hear from him again.’       “‘Done!’ said he. ‘We picked him out of the gutter, and he won’t
      leave us so easily.’ Those were his very words.”       “‘The impudent scoundrel!’ I cried. ‘I’ve never so much as seen
      him in my life. Why should I consider him in any way? I shall
      certainly not write if you would rather I didn’t.’
