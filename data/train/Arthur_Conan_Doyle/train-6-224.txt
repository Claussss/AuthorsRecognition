A moment later with flushed face he was in the street and pouring out
his thanks to Aylward. “Alas, my fair lord! the man has robbed us now,” said the archer. “We
could have had another twenty had we stood fast.” “How know you that, good Aylward?” “By his eyes, Squire Loring. I wot I have little store of reading where
the parchment of a book or the pinching of a blazon is concerned, but I
can read men's eyes, and I never doubted that he would give what he has
given.”
