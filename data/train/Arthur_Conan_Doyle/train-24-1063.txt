“By Saint George!” cried Sir Hugh, “I have never seen signs of so stern
a fight, and I am right glad that we have been in time to save you.” “You have saved more than us,” said John, pointing to the banner which
leaned against the rock behind him. “You have done nobly,” cried the old free companion, gazing with a
soldier's admiration at the huge frame and bold face of the archer. “But
why is it, my good fellow, that you sit upon this man.”
