“What?” cried Aylward. “Am I to be left behind? I, who am the Squire's
own man? Ill fare the bowman who comes betwixt me and yonder boat!” “Nay, Aylward,” said his master, “I order that you stay, for indeed you
are a sick man.” “But now that the waves have sunk I am myself again. Nay, fair sir, I
pray that you will not leave me behind.” “You must needs take the space of a better man; for what do you know of
the handling of a boat?” said Badding shortly. “No more fool's talk, I
pray you, for the night will soon fall. Stand aside!”
