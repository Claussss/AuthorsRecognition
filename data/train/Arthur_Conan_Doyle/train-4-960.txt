‘We are fighting against the men with the red coats, my chuck,’ said
I; ‘we shall take care of your house with you, and let no one steal
anything.’ ‘Nay, then ye may stay,’ quoth she, climbing up upon my knee as grave as
a sparrow upon a bough. ‘What a great boy you are!’ ‘And why not a man?’ I asked. ‘Because you have no beard upon your face. Why, granny hath more hair
upon her chin than you. Besides, only boys drink milk. Men drink cider.’
