"Oh, uncle, here's a regiment of soldiers coming down the street with
the band playing in front of them." "A ridgment, eh?  Where be my glasses?  Lor, but I can hear the band,
as plain as plain!  Here's the pioneers an' the drum-major!  What be
their number, lass?"  His eyes were shining and his bony yellow
fingers, like the claws of some fierce old bird, dug into her shoulder. "They don't seem to have no number, uncle.  They've something wrote on
their shoulders.  Oxfordshire, I think it be."
