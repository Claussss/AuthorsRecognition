Chandos and Nigel looked ruefully at each other. Then the knight patted
his Squire upon his bandaged head. “You have carried yourself right well, Nigel. I could not wish for
better. Fear not. All will be well.” “My fair and honored lord,” cried Nigel, “I am heavy at heart, for
indeed I could do no other, and yet I have brought trouble upon you.” “Nay, the clouds will soon pass. If he does indeed slay this Frenchman,
you have done all that lay within your power, and your mind may rest
easy.”
