“There is one of our Frenchmen at the wheel. We had best put the mate
overboard,” said the steward. “That is safest,” said Captain Fourneau. But that was more than I could stand. Nothing would persuade me to agree
to the death of a helpless man. With a bad grace Captain Fourneau consented to spare him, and we carried
him to the after-hold, which lay under the cabin. There he was laid
among the bales of Manchester cloth.
