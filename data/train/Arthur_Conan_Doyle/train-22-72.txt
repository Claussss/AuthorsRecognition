The sergeant picked up a card which lay beside the dead man on the
floor. The initials V.V. and under them the number 341 were rudely
scrawled in ink upon it. “What's this?” he asked, holding it up. Barker looked at it with curiosity. “I never noticed it before,” he
said. “The murderer must have left it behind him.” “V.V.--341. I can make no sense of that.” The sergeant kept turning it over in his big fingers. “What's V.V.?
Somebody's initials, maybe. What have you got there, Dr. Wood?”
