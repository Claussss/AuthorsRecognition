“My fair sir, you flatter me,” cried the mayor. “I am a peaceful trader,
and I am not wont to be so shouted at upon so small a matter.” “Small!” shrieked the other. “Small! Clams and scallops! Ask me to your
table to partake of the dainty of the town, and when I come a barren
welcome and a bare board! Where is my spear-bearer?” “Nay, Sir Oliver, Sir Oliver!” cried Sir Nigel, laughing. “Let your anger be appeased, since instead of this dish you come upon an
old friend and comrade.”
