“I’ve got a good deal to say,” our prisoner said slowly. “I want to tell
you gentlemen all about it.” “Hadn’t you better reserve that for your trial?” asked the Inspector. “I may never be tried,” he answered. “You needn’t look startled. It
isn’t suicide I am thinking of. Are you a Doctor?” He turned his fierce
dark eyes upon me as he asked this last question. “Yes; I am,” I answered. “Then put your hand here,” he said, with a smile, motioning with his
manacled wrists towards his chest.
