The moment of fate had arrived. “You are Colonel Gerard?” said the terrible old man. “Aide-de-camp to the robber who calls himself General Suchet, who in
turn represents that arch-robber Buonaparte?” It was on my lips to tell him that he was a liar, but there is a time to
argue and a time to be silent. “I am an honourable soldier,” said I. “I have obeyed my orders and done
my duty.” The blood flushed into the old man's face and his eyes blazed through
his mask.
