“I thank you all for your very gentle courtesy,” said a booming voice
from the figure upon the ground. At the words even the valiant Manny sprang into his saddle. Some rode
madly away from the horrid trunk. A few of the boldest lingered. “Most of all,” said the voice, “would I thank the most noble knight,
Sir Walter Manny, that he should deign to lay aside his greatness and
condescend to do a deed of arms upon so humble a Squire.”
