“What boots it to wait?” said Sir William Felton. “Let us ride down upon
their camp ere they discover us.” “And so say I,” cried the Scottish earl; “for they do not know that
there is any enemy within thirty long leagues of them.” “For my part,” said Sir Simon Burley, “I think that it is madness, for
you cannot hope to rout this great army; and where are you to go and
what are you to do when they have turned upon you? How say you, Sir
Oliver Buttesthorn?”
