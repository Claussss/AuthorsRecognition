“Might it not be broiled, most learned sir?” asked the tooth-drawer. “A
raw rat sounds a most sorry and cheerless dish.” “Not to be eaten,” cried the physician, in high disdain. “Why should any
man eat such a thing?” “Why indeed?” asked the gleeman, taking a long drain at his tankard. “It is to be placed on the sore or swelling. For the rat, mark you,
being a foul-living creature, hath a natural drawing or affinity for
all foul things, so that the noxious humors pass from the man into the
unclean beast.”
