‘Go ‘way!’ she cried, still waving her hands and shaking her kerchief.
‘Go ‘way! Granny told me to tell any one that came to go ‘way!’ ‘But if they would not go away, little mistress,’ asked Reuben, ‘what
were you to do then?’ ‘I was to drive them ‘way,’ she answered, advancing boldly against us
with many flaps. ‘You bad man!’ she continued, flashing out at me, ‘you
have broken granny’s bolt.’ ‘Nay, I’ll mend it again,’ I answered penitently, and catching up
a stone I soon fastened the injured staple. ‘There, mistress, your
granddam will never tell the difference.’
