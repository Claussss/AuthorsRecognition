‘Keep a look-out there, Tom Baldock!’ cried Dicon to a man in the bows.
‘We are in the track of every Bristol ship, and though there’s so little
wind, a high-sparred craft might catch a breeze which we miss.’ ‘Sh!’ said Long John suddenly, holding up his hand in warning. ‘Sh!’ We listened with all our ears, but there was no sound, save the gentle
wash of the unseen waves against our sides. ‘Call the mate!’ whispered the seaman. ‘There’s a craft close by us. I
heard the rattle of a rope upon her deck.’
