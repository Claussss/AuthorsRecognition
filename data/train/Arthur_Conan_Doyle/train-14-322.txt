      “I have the pleasure,” said I, “of knowing your father.”       It was a clumsy introduction, and the lady made me feel it.
      “There is nothing in common between my father and me,” she said.
      “I owe him nothing, and his friends are not mine. If it were not
      for the late Sir Charles Baskerville and some other kind hearts I
      might have starved for all that my father cared.”       “It was about the late Sir Charles Baskerville that I have come
      here to see you.”
