“What has happened?” I cried. “What is amiss, Mordaunt?” “My father!” he gasped--“my father!” His hat was gone, his eyes dilated with terror, and his face as
bloodless as that of a corpse. I could feel that the hands which clasped
my arms were quivering and shaking with emotion. “You are exhausted,” I said, leading him into the parlour. “Give
yourself a moment's rest before you speak to us. Be calm, man, you are
with your best friends.”
