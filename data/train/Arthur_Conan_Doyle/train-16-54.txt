"Then you go--you really go?" said she. "My studies call me." "And to Gaster Fell?" she asked. "Yes; to the cottage which I have built there." "And you will live alone there?" "With my hundred companions who lie in that cart." "Ah, books!" she cried, with a pretty shrug of her graceful shoulders.
"But you will make me a promise?" "What is it?" I asked, in surprise. "It is a small thing. You will not refuse me?"
