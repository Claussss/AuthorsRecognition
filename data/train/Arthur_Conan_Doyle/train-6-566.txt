“A thousand will suffice, fair sir, to pay my charges for the war. You
will not again play the spy, nor do us harm until the truce is broken.” “That I will swear.” “And lastly there is a journey that you shall make.” The Frenchman's face lengthened. “Where you order I must go,” said he;
“but I pray you that it is not to the Holy Land.” “Nay,” said Nigel; “but it is to a land which is holy to me. You will
make your way back to Southampton.”
