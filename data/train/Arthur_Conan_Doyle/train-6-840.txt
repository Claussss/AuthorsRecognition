“It is to the Lady Mary, daughter of old Sir John Buttesthorn who dwells
near Guildford.” “But you will write the message, Nigel. Such greetings as a cavalier
sends to his lady-love should be under seal.” “Nay, he can carry my message by word of mouth.” “Then I shall tell him for he goes this morning. What message, then,
shall he say to the lady?” “He will give her my very humble greeting, and he will say to her that
for the second time Saint Catharine has been our friend.”
