Know’st thou not, Bertram, What she has done for me? Yes, my good lord, But never hope to know why I should marry her. Thou know’st she has raised me from my sickly bed. But follows it, my lord, to bring me down
Must answer for your raising? I know her well:
She had her breeding at my father’s charge.
A poor physician’s daughter, my wife? Disdain
Rather corrupt me ever.
