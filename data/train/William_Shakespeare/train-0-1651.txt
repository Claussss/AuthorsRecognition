Marcus, even thou hast struck upon my crest,
And with these boys mine honour thou hast
wounded.
My foes I do repute you every one,
So trouble me no more, but get you gone. He is not with himself, let us withdraw. Not I, till Mutius’ bones be buried. Marcus, Lucius, Quintus, and Martius kneel Brother, for in that name doth nature plead—
