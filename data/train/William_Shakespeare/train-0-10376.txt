CORIOLANUS unmuffling his head If, Tullus, Not yet thou know’st me, and seeing me dost not
Think me for the man I am, necessity
Commands me name myself. AUFIDIUS What is thy name? A name unmusical to the Volscians’ ears
And harsh in sound to thine. Say, what’s thy name? Thou hast a grim appearance, and thy face
Bears a command in’t. Though thy tackle’s torn,
Thou show’st a noble vessel. What’s thy name?
