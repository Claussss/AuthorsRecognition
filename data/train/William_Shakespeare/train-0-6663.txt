HAMLET What man dost thou dig it for? FIRST CLOWN For no man, sir. HAMLET What woman, then? FIRST CLOWN For none, neither. HAMLET Who is to be buried in’t? FIRST CLOWN One that was a woman, sir; but, rest her soul, she’s dead. HAMLET How absolute the knave is! We must speak by the card, or equivocation will undo us. By the Lord, Horatio, these three years I have taken note of it. The age is grown so picked that the toe of the peasant comes so near the heel of the courtier he galls his kibe. (To the First Clown) How long hast thou been a grave-maker?
