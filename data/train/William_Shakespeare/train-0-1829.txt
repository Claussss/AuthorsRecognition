He stabs the Empress Die, frantic wretch, for this accursed deed. He kills Titus Can the son’s eye behold his father bleed?
There’s meed for meed, death for a deadly deed. He kills Saturninus. Confusion follows.
Enter Goths. Lucius, Marcus and others go aloft You sad-faced men, people and sons of Rome,
By uproars severed, as a flight of fowl
Scattered by winds and high tempestuous gusts,
O, let me teach you how to knit again
This scattered corn into one mutual sheaf,
These broken limbs again into one body.
