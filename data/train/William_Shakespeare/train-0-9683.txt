PAROLES You had my prayers to lead them on, and to keep them on have them stitt.—O my knave, how does my old lady? LAVATCH So that you had her wrinkles and I her money, I would she did as you say. PAROLES Why, I say nothing. LAVATCH Marry, you are the wiser man, for many a man’s tongue shakes out his master’s undoing. To say nothing, to do nothing, to know nothing, and to have nothing, is to be a great part of your title, which is within a very little of nothing.
