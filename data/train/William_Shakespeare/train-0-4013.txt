HIPPOLYTA Beshrew my heart, but I pity the man. BOTTOM (as Pyramus) O wherefore, nature, didst thou lions frame,
Since lion vile hath here deflowered my dear?—
Which is—no, no, which was—the fairest dame That lived, that loved, that liked, that looked, with
cheer.
Come tears, confound;
Out sword, and wound
The pap of Pyramus.
Ay, that left pap,
Where heart doth hop.
Thus die I: thus, thus, thus.
He stabs himself
Now am I dead,
Now am I fled,
My soul is in the sky.
Tongue, lose thy light;
Moon, take thy flight. Exit Moonshine
Now die, die, die, die, die. He dies
