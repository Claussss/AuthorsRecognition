Sc. 5
Enter King Lear, the Earl of Kent disguised, and Lear’s Fool 
LEAR to Kent KENT I will not sleep, my lord, till I have delivered your letter. FOOL If a man’s brains were in his heels, were’t not in danger of kibes? LEAR Ay, boy. FOOL Then, I prithee, be merry: thy wit shall ne’er go slipshod. LEAR Ha, ha, ha!
