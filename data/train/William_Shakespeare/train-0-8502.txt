OSWALD I am none of this, my lord, I beseech you pardon me. LEAR Do you bandy looks with me, you rascal? Lear strikes him OSWALD I’ll not be struck, my lord— KENT (tripping him) Nor tripped neither, you base football player. LEAR (to Kent) I thank thee, fellow. Thou serv’st me, and I’ll love thee. KENT (to Oswald) Come, sir, I’ll teach you differences. Away, away. If you will measure your lubber’s length again, tarry; but away if you have wisdom.
