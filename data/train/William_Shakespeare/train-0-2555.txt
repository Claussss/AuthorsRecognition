And turned it thus: ‘It cannot be, I find,
But such a face should bear a wicked mind. ‘For even as subtle Sinon here is painted,
So sober-sad, so weary, and so mild,
As if with grief or travail he had fainted,
To me came Tarquin armed, too beguiled
With outward honesty, but yet defiled With inward vice. As Priam him did cherish,
So did I Tarquin, so my Troy did perish.
