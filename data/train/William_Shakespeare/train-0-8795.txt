POET That’s not feigned, he is so. APEMANTUS Yes, he is worthy of thee, and to pay thee for thy labour. He that loves to be flattered is worthy o’th’ flatterer. Heavens, that I were a lord! TIMON What wouldst do then, Apemantus? APEMANTUS E’en as Apemantus does now: hate a lord with my heart. TIMON What, thyself?
