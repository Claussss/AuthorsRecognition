In vain thou speak’st, poor boy. My father’s blood
Hath stopped the passage where thy words should
enter. Then let my father’s blood open it again.
He is a man, and, Clifford, cope with him. Had I thy brethren here, their lives and thine
Were not revenge sufficient for me.
No—if I digged up thy forefathers’ graves,
And hung their rotten coffins up in chains,
It could not slake mine ire nor ease my heart.
The sight of any of the house of York
Is as a fury to torment my soul.
And till I root out their accursed line,
And leave not one alive, I live in hell.
Therefore—
