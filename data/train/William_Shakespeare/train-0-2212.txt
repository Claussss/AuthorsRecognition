STANLEY (bearing the crown) Courageous Richmond, well hast thou acquit thee.
Lo, here this long usurp
From the dead temples of this bloody wretch
Have I plucked off, to grace thy brows withal.
Wear it, enjoy it, and make much of it. He sets the crown on Henry’s head KING HENRY THE SEVENTH Great God of heaven, say ‘Amen’ to all.
But tell me—young George Stanley, is he living?
