Enter Edgar Pat he comes, like the catastrophe of the old comedy. My cue is villainous melancholy, with a sigh like Tom o’ Bedlam. He reads a book —O, these eclipses do portend these divisions. Fa, so, la, mi. EDGAR How now, brother Edmond, what serious contemplation are you in? EDMOND I am thinking, brother, of a prediction I read this other day, what should follow these eclipses.
