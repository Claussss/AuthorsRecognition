SIR ANDREW (to Feste) There’s a testril of me, too. If one knight give a— FESTE Would you have a love-song, or a song of good life? SIR TOBY A love song, a love-song. SIR ANDREW Ay, ay. I care not for good life. FESTE (sings) O mistress mine, where are you roaming?
O stay and hear, your true love’s coming,
That can sing both high and low.
Trip no further, pretty sweeting.
Journeys end in lovers meeting,
Every wise man’s son doth know.
