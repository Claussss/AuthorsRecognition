WILLIAM Ay, sir, I thank God. TOUCHSTONE Thank God—a good answer. Art rich? WILLIAM Faith, sir, so-so. TOUCHSTONE So-so is good, very good, very excellent good. And yet it is not, it is but so-so. Art thou wise? WILLIAM Ay, sir, I have a pretty wit. TOUCHSTONE Why, thou sayst well. I do now remember a saying: ‘The fool doth think he is wise, but the wise man knows himself to be a fool.’ The heathen philosopher, when he had a desire to eat a grape, would open his lips when he put it into his mouth, meaning thereby that grapes were made to eat, and lips to open. You do love this maid?
