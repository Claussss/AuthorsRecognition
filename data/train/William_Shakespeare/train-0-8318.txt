OTHELLO Hath he said anything? He hath, my lord. But, be you well assured,
No more than he’ll unswear. OTHELLO What hath he said? Faith, that he did—I know not what he did. What, what? OTHELLO With her? IAGO With her, on her, what you will. OTHELLO Lie with her? Lie on her? We say ‘lie on her’ when they belie her. Lie with her? ’Swounds, that’s fulsome! Handkerchief—confessions—hankerchief. To confess and be hanged for his labour. First to be hanged and then to confess! I tremble at it. Nature would not invest herself in such shadowing passion without some instruction. It is not words that shakes me thus. Pish! Noses, ears, and lips! Is’t possible? Confess? Handkerchief? O devil!
