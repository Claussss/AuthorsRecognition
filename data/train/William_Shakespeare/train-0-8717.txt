LEAR Do not abuse me. Be comforted, good madam. The great rage
You see is cured in him, and yet it is danger
To make him even o’er the time he has lost.
Desire him to go in; trouble him no more
Till further settling. CORDELIA (to Lear) Will’t please your highness walk? LEAR You must bear with me. Pray now, forget and forgive. I am old
And foolish. Exeunt all but Kent and [First] Gentleman
