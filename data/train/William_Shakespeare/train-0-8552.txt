What, art thou mad, old fellow? GLOUCESTER to Kent No contraries hold more antipathy
Than I and such a knave. CORNWALL Why dost thou call him knave? What’s his offence? KENT His countenance likes me not. No more perchance does mine, or his, or hers. Sir, ’tis my occupation to be plain:
I have seen better faces in my time
Than stands on any shoulder that I see
Before me at this instant.
