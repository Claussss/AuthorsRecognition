SIR JOHN No, by my troth, not so much as will serve to be prologue to an egg and butter. PRINCE HARRY Well, how then? Come, roundly, roundly. SIR JOHN Marry then, sweet wag, when thou art king let not us that are squires of the night’s body be called thieves of the day’s beauty. Let us be ‘Diana’s foresters’, ‘gentlemen of the shade’, ‘minions of the moon’, and let men say we be men of good government, being governed, as the sea is, by our noble and chaste mistress the moon, under whose countenance we steal.
