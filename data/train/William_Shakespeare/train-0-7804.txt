ALL THE REST Fire the houses, fire the houses! DOLL Ay, for we may as well make bonfires on May Day as at Midsummer. We’ll alter the day in the calendar, and set it down in flaming letters. Stay, that would much endanger the whole city,
Whereto I would not the least prejudice. DOLL No, nor I neither: so may mine own house be burned for company. I’ll tell ye what: we’ll drag the strangers out into Moorfields, and there bumbaste them till they stink again.
