Enter Prince Hamlet and Horatio So much for this, sir. Now, let me see, the other.
You do remember all the circumstance? HORATIO Remember it, my lord! Sir, in my heart there was a kind of fighting
That would not let me sleep. Methought I lay
Worse than the mutines in the bilboes. Rashly—
And praised be rashness for it: let us know
Our indiscretion sometime serves us well
When our dear plots do pall, and that should teach us
There’s a divinity that shapes our ends,
Rough-hew them how we will—
