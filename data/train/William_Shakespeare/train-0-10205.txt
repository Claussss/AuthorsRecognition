So, to our tent, Where, ere we do repose us, we will write
To Rome of our success. You, Titus Lartius,
Must to Corioles back. Send us to Rome
The best, with whom we may articulate
For their own good and ours. I shall, my lord. The gods begin to mock me. I, that now Refused most princely gifts, am bound to beg
Of my lord general.
