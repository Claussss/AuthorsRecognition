Put not your worthy rage into your tongue.
One time will owe another. CORIOLANUS On fair ground I could beat forty of them. MENENIUS I could myself Take up a brace o’th’ best of them, yea, the two
tribunes. But now ‘tis odds beyond arithmetic,
And manhood is called foolery when it stands
Against a falling fabric.
(To Coriolanus) Will you hence
Before the tag return, whose rage doth rend
Like interrupted waters, and o’erbear
What they are used to bear?
