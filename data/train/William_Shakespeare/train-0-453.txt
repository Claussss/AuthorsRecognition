HORTENSIO Marry, sir, to get a husband for her sister. GREMIO A husband?—a devil! HORTENSIO I say a husband. GREMIO I say a devil. Think’st thou, Hortensio, though her father be very rich, any man is so very a fool to be married to hell? HORTENSIO Tush, Gremio. Though it pass your patience and mine to endure her loud alarums, why, man, there be good fellows in the world, an a man could light on them, would take her with all faults, and money enough.
