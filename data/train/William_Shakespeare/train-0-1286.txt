Enter the Earl of Oxford, with a drummer and soldiers bearingcolours O cheerful colours! See where Oxford comes. Oxford, Oxford, for Lancaster! Oxford and his men pass over the stage and exeunt into the city RICHARD OF GLOUCESTER (to King Edward) The gates are open—let us enter too. KING EDWARD So other foes may set upon our backs?
Stand we in good array, for they no doubt
Will issue out again and bid us battle.
If not, the city being but of small defence,
We’ll quickly rouse the traitors in the same.
