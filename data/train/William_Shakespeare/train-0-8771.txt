POET (to himself) ‘When we for recompense have praised the vile,
It stains the glory in that happy verse
Which aptly sings the good.’ MERCHANT (to Jeweller) ’Tis a good form. And rich. Here is a water, look ye. PAINTER (to Poet) You are rapt, sir, in some work, some dedication
To the great lord. POET A thing slipped idly from me.
