SICINIUS On the sudden I warrant him consul. BRUTUS Then our office may During his power go sleep. He cannot temp’rately transport his honours
From where he should begin and end, but will
Lose those he hath won. BRUTUS In that there’s comfort. SICINIUS Doubt not The commoners, for whom we stand, but they
Upon their ancient malice will forget
With the least cause these his new honours, which
That he will give them make I as little question
As he is proud to do’t.
